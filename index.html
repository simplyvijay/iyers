Iyer community

Origin of Hinduism can be related to Satavahana Kingdom who were Kings responsible for architecting Hinduism. Later a group of people split from Satavahana Kingdom and formed Pallava Kingdom (Pallava in Sanskrit 'Branch') who were the origin of Shaivam. The Shaivaite Pallava kings changed themselves to missionaries by leading a life with a rationale of Shaivam giving birth to Shaivaite brahmins. The success of Shaivam was challenged by the growing emotional influence by other religions, as a result, Adi Shankara (A Shaivaite brahmin) established 'Self' & emotionless life all over the country marking the beginning of Iyer community.


British rule and aftermath

After the arrival of British in India, the Iyers divided into two groups, one that directly opposed British rule and the other working along British to establish the science perspective in India thereby indirectly contributing to the country's confidence for Independent India. Notable people in the community are CV Raman (Nobel Prize in Physics), Subramanyam Chandrashekhar (Nobel Prize in Physics) and Venkatraman Ramakrishnan (Nobel Prize in Chemistry), Subramania Bharathi (Pro-Independence leader), U Ve Swaminatha Iyer (Archivist), Meenakshi Ammal (Connoisseur of food), M S Subbulakshmi (Carnatic artiste), Padma Subramanyam (Bharatanatyam artiste) among others. Iyers were responsible for establishing a strong growth foundation in India and are the mainstream community of the country. The community has a tradition of pioneering establishments in India as in media (Indhiya, Swadesamitran, The Hindu, Tughlaq, Dinamalar etc.), Universities (IISc etc.) and industries thereby contributing to change in people's perspective towards growth and independence.
